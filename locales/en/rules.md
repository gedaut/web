# Basic Provisions

## Name of the Sport

The sport is called Gedaut.

## Objective of the Game

The objective is to be the last person remaining in the pool.

---

# Game Equipment

## Pool

The pool must be circular with a diameter of approximately 3 m to 9 m.

The pool must not contain any dangerous or sharp parts.

## Ball

The ball must be spherical with a diameter of approximately 15 cm to 30 cm.

The ball must be made of water-resistant material and inflated to a reasonable pressure. The ball must not cause injury, even during hard shots.

## Player Equipment

### Mandatory Equipment

* Swimsuit bottoms.

### Prohibited Equipment

* Jewelry, watches, fitness trackers, and other solid or sharp objects.
* Any objects that could mechanically damage the ball or the walls of the pool.
* Any equipment designed to influence the trajectory of the ball in favor of a given *player*.

---

# Definitions

## *Player*

A natural person and their equipment playing in the current *game*.

## *Participant*

A natural person participating in the broadest context of the competition.

## *Round*

A round consists of individual games and the breaks between them.

* **Start:** The start of the first game.
* **End:** One or zero *players* remain in the pool.

## *Game*

The basic game unit. *Game* states, actions, and fouls are evaluated exclusively during a game.

* **Start:** Commences with a throw-in, unless specified otherwise.
* **End:** The elimination of a *player* from the round or a technical interruption. *Players* lose their active states.

---

# *Game* States

## Immunity

* **Condition:** If a *player* is immune, they must not be designated by the last touch rule; the consequence is the end of the *game*.
* **Termination:** The ball touches the water surface.

## Frozen

* **Condition:** If a *player* is frozen, they must not touch the ball with an upper limb.
* **Termination:** The ball touches the pool wall or another *player*.

---

# *Game* Actions

## Last Touch

* **Condition:** The *player* who last touched the ball before it leaves the pool or stops outside the water without contacting a *player* is designated.
* **Consequence:** This *player* is eliminated from the *round*, unless specified otherwise. In the event of a disputed touch, the *game* may be replayed.

## Throw-in

The default method of putting the ball into *play*.

**Condition:** The referee or a designated person puts the ball into *play* by throwing it from a spot outside the pool so that the ball touches the water surface inside the pool. The *game* begins at the moment of this touch. The designated person may be someone who was eliminated in the previous game.

## Opening Throw (Serve)

Starting the *game* by throwing the ball.

* **Condition:** The main serving *player* must throw the ball with sufficient speed so that it flies approximately through the center of the pool.
* **Consequence:** All *players*, unless specified otherwise, gain the immunity state upon the throw. All serving *players* gain the frozen state immediately after the throw.

## Hit

The ball touches *player* A, then *player* B excluding the hands (from the wrist down), and then the water surface without any intermediate touch.

* **Consequence:** *Player* B gains the frozen state.

## Shielding

*Player* A touches the ball with their hand and simultaneously touches *player* B. Both touches must occur above the water surface.

* **Consequence:** Player B gains the immunity state.

# *Game* Fouls

## *Players*

* **Fouling player:** The *player* who committed the foul.
* **Fouled player:** The *player* who was restricted by the foul. Unless stated otherwise for a specific foul, the fouled players are all other *players* in the *game*.

## Types

* **Bad throw:** Violation of the condition of the opening throw action.
* **Touching immunity:** Violation of the condition of one's own immunity state.
* **Prohibited unfreezing:** Violation of the condition of one's own frozen state.
* **Moving with the ball:** A *player* swims, walks, or runs with the ball in an upper limb.
* **Delaying the *game*:** A *player* submerges for a long time or holds the ball in their hand (the limit is approximately 5 seconds).
* **Illegal contact:** A *player* holds, pushes, presses, or otherwise actively restricts an opponent's movement. The fouled *player* is the one restricted by this foul.
* **Splashing water:** A *player* intentionally splashes water into an opponent's eyes. The fouled *player* is the one restricted by this foul.

## Penalties

**Immediate consequence:** Every fouled *player* gains the immunity state.
**End of *game*:** The *game* ends when the ball touches the water surface. If all fouled *players* fouled at the same time, the *game* continues without change.
**Consequence:** During the opening throw of the next *game* in the given *round*, the fouled *players* have immunity. The first non-eliminated fouling *player* becomes the main server, and the other fouling players become servers.
**Higher penalty:** An intentional foul results in the elimination of the fouling *player* from the given *round*.

---

# Disciplinary Fouls

Disciplinary fouls are assessed and penalized regardless of whether a *game* or *round* is in progress.

## Types

* **Dangerous behavior:** A *participant* intentionally or through gross negligence endangers the health of persons or damages game equipment.
* **Obstructing the *game*:** A *participant* intentionally disrupts or delays the start, progress, or end of a *game* (e.g., refusing to serve).
* **Unsportsmanlike conduct:** A *participant* violates fair play, provokes or insults others, or deliberately disrupts the sporting conduct of the competition.

## Penalties

* **Warning:** The *participant* is warned.
* **Elimination:** If the *participant* is in a *round*, they are immediately eliminated from the *round*.
* **Higher penalties:** Penalties extending beyond the scope of the current *round*. The competition organizer has the right to define and impose them.
